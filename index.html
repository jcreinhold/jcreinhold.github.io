I’m an electrical engineering Ph.D. student at Johns Hopkins University working under Professor Jerry Prince in the Image Analysis and Communication Lab. My research interests are in medical image analysis and I am currently working on anomaly detection and image synthesis as related to MR images of the brain. I am a firm believer in creating reproducible research, which includes the creation of very usable and well-documented tools.

Before starting graduate school I worked on a variety of problems. Here are some examples: lesion detectability in digital breast tomosynthesis images and HF angle of arrival determination.
